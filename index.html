01 / ABOUT

The work

I study how organizations design decisions under uncertainty — when outcomes depend on the architecture of information, choices, and the analytics that support them.

I am an Associate Professor (with tenure) of Information Technology and Operations Management at Florida Atlantic University. I received my Ph.D. in Business (Business Information Technology) from Virginia Tech in 2018, with a secondary major in Disaster Risk Management.

My work combines analytical modeling, empirical analysis, and advanced analytics, with applications in resilient operations, humanitarian systems, collective-action environments, and AI-enabled decision making. It has appeared in Production and Operations Management, European Journal of Operational Research, Decision Sciences, Risk Analysis, and Regional Studies. In 2023, I was named the College of Business Scholar of the Year.

02 / RESEARCH

Areas of inquiry

→ DIRECTION 01

Information Design

How disclosure, transparency, and communication policies shape participation and coordination in volunteer and collective-action systems — information as an endogenous operational choice.

→ DIRECTION 02

Donation-Menu Design

Reframing restricted giving as a design problem: how nonprofits can structure donation menus to balance donor preferences, funding flexibility, and operational performance.

→ DIRECTION 03

Analytics & AI

Deploying analytics and AI for organizational decisions — including retrieval-augmented enterprise AI under adversarial risk and trustworthy human–AI decision support.

→ FOUNDATION

Supply Chain Resilience

Published work on sourcing under disruption risk, firm-level impacts of disruptions, and the performance implications of operational strategies.

→ FOUNDATION

Public-Sector Resilience

Pandemic response coordination, epidemic investment, and equity–efficiency tradeoffs in critical infrastructure planning.

→ METHODS

Pluralistic Toolkit

Optimization, stochastic and robust modeling, empirical methods, network analysis, and large-scale computational analytics.
