— RESEARCH · DECISION DESIGN UNDER UNCERTAINTY

The architecture of decisions.

I study how organizations design decisions under uncertainty — when outcomes depend jointly on information, human behavior, operational constraints, and data. My work sits at the intersection of operations management, information systems, and business analytics.

00 / THEME

Unifying idea

— ORGANIZING QUESTION
Performance depends not only on resources and incentives, but on the architecture of decisions — what information is disclosed, what choices are offered, how actors respond, and how data and analytics support the resulting decisions. How should organizations design information, choices, and analytical systems to improve decision quality under uncertainty?

02 / CURRENT

Information design

— DIRECTION ONE
COLLECTIVE-ACTION · VOLUNTEER SYSTEMS

How information disclosure shapes participation and coordination in decentralized systems. Outcomes depend not only on incentives, but on what participants know about others, when they know it, and how disclosure affects belief and behavior — information as an endogenous design variable.

ONGOING
Strategic Information Disclosure in Volunteer Mobilization
How disclosure policies — what volunteers see about others' participation, when, and how — influence volunteer behavior and system-level outcomes in collective-action settings.
FORWARD
Disclosure, transparency & coordination on digital platforms
Bridging operations management, information systems, and applied microeconomic theory — treating information structure as an operational decision variable in volunteer platforms, nonprofit operations, and other digitally mediated environments.
03 / CURRENT

Donation-menu design

— DIRECTION TWO
HUMANITARIAN FUNDING · NONPROFIT OPS

How choice architectures can balance donor preferences with organizational flexibility. Donors prefer earmarked giving; organizations need discretion to reallocate as needs evolve. Reframing restricted giving as a design problem rather than an exogenous constraint.

WORKING PAPER
Donation Menu Design Under Earmarking: Endogenizing Flexibility in Humanitarian Funding
How nonprofits can actively design donation menus to influence donor choice and improve downstream allocation — studying the interaction between donor heterogeneity, menu structure, funding flexibility, and operational performance.
FORWARD
Analytical & institutional grounding in humanitarian funding systems
Deepening the stream by integrating analytical models with richer empirical and institutional evidence on humanitarian and nonprofit funding mechanisms — where donor preferences, operational uncertainty, and restricted funding interact.
04 / CURRENT

Analytics & AI

— DIRECTION THREE
AI-ENABLED DECISION SYSTEMS

Analytics and AI as a core capability — not a side interest — that unifies my research. From mobility data and IRS 990 records to retrieval-augmented enterprise AI, I study how organizations can deploy analytics effectively while managing vulnerabilities around data quality, security, robustness, and trust.

WORKING PAPER
Risk-Based Security Investment for Retrieval-Augmented Enterprise AI under Data Poisoning
How organizations should allocate security investment across retrieval-augmented AI pipelines facing adversarial data-poisoning threats — linking operations and decision-support design with emerging AI vulnerabilities.
PUBLISHED
Adaptive cyber disaster management
Designing adaptive recovery and response processes for organizations facing cyber disasters — a bridge from my resilience work into AI-enabled, cyber-physical decision settings.
FORWARD
Trustworthy decision systems: governance, reliability, human–AI interaction
Studying the settings where algorithmic support and human judgment interact — model reliability, governance, and the design of decision-support systems that are effective, secure, trustworthy, and resilient.
